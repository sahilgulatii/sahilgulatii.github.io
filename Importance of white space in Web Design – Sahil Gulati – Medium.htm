Importance of white space in Web Design

I believe using the right amount of white/negative space is a crucial part of web design. It makes the webpage readable and more user friendly. There is really no excuse for using less space than desired on a webpage. In a Poster or handmade design there are constrains like shortage of paper or ink but on web you can use all the space in the world.

I came across the website for this Tennis Tournament called Laver Cup. I’ll talk about the website later in this article. The inaugural season of this tournament was held this year. 83,273 people attended the five sessions of Tennis played in Prague. Members of the winning team got $250,000 in prize money. The highlight of the tournament was a doubles match with two huge names in Tennis- Roger Federer and Rafael Nadal on the same side. After the success this year, the organizers promised to come back with an even bigger season next year in Chicago. After visiting their website, I was surprised to see a major blunder in the design.

Problem

The colors used and the layout seem to look pretty decent. But a user would have a hard time finding what teams won the games, which is a very important part of this website. It is difficult to figure out what games are the scores referring to.

“3PTS — EUROPE WON” is the result for the game below and not the one above it. As can be seen from the image above, EUROPE (BERDYCH/ CILIC) did not win this game; the other team did.

Solution

A solution would be to simply increase the space between the result and the games. I made this change and it was very effective.

This proves that good use of space can improve the UX of the website and make it more user friendly.
